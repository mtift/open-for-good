Open for Good

Your mission matters more than your software budget.

If you run a nonprofit, a community group, or a small organization that exists to help people, you probably spend more on software than you’d like. Subscriptions for email, donor management, office tools, and website hosting add up fast. For organizations running on grants and donations, every dollar spent on software licenses is a dollar that doesn’t go toward the work.

There’s another way. Free and open source software (sometimes called FLOSS) can handle most of what nonprofits need, from managing donors to building websites to writing reports. And “free” here means two things: free as in no cost, and free as in freedom. You can use it, study it, share it, and change it. No one can take it away from you or raise the price next year.

This site exists to help people doing good in the world find and use free software with confidence. You don’t need a tech background. You don’t need an IT department. You just need to know where to look and how to get started.

What you’ll find here

The Software Guide walks through free tools organized by the jobs nonprofits need done: managing donors, handling finances, communicating with supporters, building a website, and more.

Getting Started helps you figure out where to begin if you’ve never used free software before, or if the idea feels overwhelming.
